Herd Immunity


A strategy presupposing containment of the pandemic by running the virus through the population until enough people
develop enough antibodies each for inverting the curve of growth in cases and deaths until the virus stops from spreading.

Herd Immunity (UK government)
Case description

The concept of herd immunity1 was first introduced in the context of the Covid-19 pandemic, by XXX, and the UK government was the first to embrace it in XXX, as a strategy to fight Covid 19. According to the UK governmentâ€™s Chief Scientific adviser, Sir Patrick Vallance, 60% of the population will need to become infected with corona virus which will leads to herd immunity. In simpler terms, this means that in a whole population, if a virus starts to spread through the population many people will get this disease and many people will also recover and they will have this immune memory that will allow them to fight the infection again. Once certain population has become immune to the disease the virus can stop circulating in the population. According to many virologists this is not a right strategy to fight Covid 19. On the other hand, UK government decided to let the herd immunity to develop within the community instead of taking necessary measures as soon as possible


Case functions

Exclusive; unintentional; sudden; top-down; regressive
The herd immunity is a strategy suddenly adopted in response to the pandemic representing the lack of government involvement in containing the virus (hence: unintentional and sudden). It is exclusive as it presupposes that the most vulnerable will have to deal with the consequences of getting affected.
