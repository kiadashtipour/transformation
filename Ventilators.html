Ventilators


This entry focuses on the invention of cheap and affordable ventilators to ease the feasibility of ventilators at the financial crisis of the current pandemic.

Improvised Ventilators (Gaza)


Case Description


A Gazan engineer, Ismail Abu Sheila, improvises a $200 ventilator to prepare for a surge of COVID-19 patients in Gaza, where health systems are nearing collapse due to a 16-year old siege (WHO report May 2019).


SDG Targets


3 (good health and wellbeing); 9 (industry, innovation, and infrastructure); 16 (peace, justice, and strong institutions)

Functions

Inclusive; deliberate; incremental; bottom-up; progressive

The initiative is inclusive for its attempt to render emergency care for Gazans residents, excluded from functioning health systems. It demonstrates a deliberation at preparing for the surge in Covid-19 cases, while being a small step towards improving access to health care. The idea and implementation is on the grassroots level, at the heart of a cluster of refugee camps consisting of 2 million people and under 12-years siege with rapidly deteriorating infrastructures. It is progressive in bringing Gazans nearer to the above-mentioned SDGs.
