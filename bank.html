World Bank Financial Aid

Case description

On April 6, 2020 World bank announced 40 Million US dollars (Contingent Emergency Response Implementation Plan) of financial aid to South Sudan.
The aid was offered to provide income for South Sudanese amid Corona Virus lockdown.
This was approved on behalf of the request given by South Sudan Government and to help the Nation in better management of the pandemic and to ensure the good working of health system.

SGD Targets

3 (Good Health and Well Being); 17 (Partnerships for the Goals); 10 (Reduced Inequalities)

Functions

Inclusive; Deliberate; Sudden; Top-down (and outside-in); Progressive
This act of World Bank is inclusive as it helped a nation with weak public health system, which anticipated to further worsen the Public health system due to the pandemic.
They deliberately addressed the financial crisis and health crisis in South Sudan by implementing financial aid for supporting low income groups and for developing public health facilities.
It is a sudden action taken by the World Bank who has been a steadfast partner in South Sudan’s effort to rebuild the health care system. It is a top-down approach by a global institution addressing a state government.
It progresses towards three SDG goals supporting lives and livelihoods with universal income and health and well-being with aid to the health system in the country.
