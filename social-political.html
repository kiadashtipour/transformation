Migrant Workers (India)



Social/Political Criticism

The entry encompasses social or political criticism of the various cases during the pandemic. Specific cases in the entry comprises cartoons, caricatures, memes and sarcastic videos.

Case description


A 48 second animated video was posted by the artist Debjyoti Saha on his Instagram account on 21/5/2020 (https://www.instagram.com/p/CAcKQ41A-s7/), depicting the disparities between the rich enjoying the lockdown, and the poor migrant workers. The video went viral with over 2 million views on Instagram and circulation on other social media platforms, Facebook (https://www.facebook.com/anuraj.manohar/videos/3325974804101605/) and on YouTube. It illustrated how a pandemic is affecting people at different levels in the society. According to the artists, “We sure have got used to this in the past four months. Being stuck at home, working without fixed hours, we are all getting used to this 'new normal'. […] However, the concept of 'home' is different to different people. For some, it is a place, a thousand miles away where they desperately want to reach, at any cost, without a penny in the pocket, a vehicle to get on or even a pair of slippers for their bleeding soles, with the hope of being at peace. […] History is witnessing one of the worst migrant crises in the country and smirking at our hypocrisy. Storms always hit the lowest of the low. Understand and acknowledge the privilege you reek of. Do what you can to ease their load. […]”

SGD Targets


1 (No Poverty), 8 (Decent Work & Economic Growth)


Functions

This video is inclusive as it illustrates the life of both middle- and upper-classes and the poor migrant workers, who lost their livelihoods all at once. This video deliberately addresses the issues faced by poor people during lockdown. It is a spontaneous video created by an artist amid lockdown. Hence a sudden response to the lives of people in different social strata during pandemic directed bottom-up, from an artist from the public. This is progressive in targeting two SDGs,
