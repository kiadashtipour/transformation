Corona Demon

The Coronavirus personified as a demon in ritual or performance, whether the motivation is religious, educational, or vocational.

Corona Demon (Mohiniyāṭṭam)

Case description


A video produced by the government of Kerala featuring the health ministry campaign “break the chain” (here) introduces an adaptation of Mohiniyattam dance to promote the hygiene and social-distancing practices required for slowing the spread of the disease. The video (here) is introduced by K. K. Shailaja, the health minister of Kerala (Malayalam with English subtitles), and by the performing artist Methil Devika (English with Malayalam subtitles).

SGD Targets


3 (health and wellbeing); 5 (gender equality); 17 (partnerships for the goals)


Functions

deliberate; sudden; top bottom, Progressive
The case addresses multiple audiences in including translations into and from Malayalam and English and in presenting the video online in social media, with the production funded by the government. It deliberates mitigating the disruption by prescribing to the public non-pharmaceutical interventions. The video is part of an on-going educational campaign on the pandemic, commissioned by Kerala State Government. It is progressive as it targets SDGs 3, 5, and 17.


Further Sources
Suanshu Khurana, “How classical dancers are bringing awareness, hope through online performances”, Indian Express, 28 April 2020

Corona Demon (Holi Festival)

Case description


During the Holi Festival, on 10 March 2020, despite the advice to avoid celebrating in public, an effigy of the Corona Demon (Coronāsur) was burned in Worli, Mumbai, with tens of thousands of people crowding for the celebration. The Coronasur was added to traditional demon effigies burned during the annual festival, that has been witnessing less traditional demons associated with evil-doing (here).


Functions

inclusive; deliberate; sudden; bottom-up; regressive

The occasion is inclusive being a shared festival, an it is also deliberate in requiring coordination, planning, and preparation. It is sudden response to the pandemic from the community, “grassroots” level. It does not address any of the SDG goals, hence regressive.

Corona Demon (Bharatanāṭyam)

Case description


The dancer Paris Laxmi performs two characters in this video, Humans vs. Corona, produced by a group of artists and practitioners. Laxmi plays a young woman who shakes hands with a visitor, as well as the Corona demon entering her house via hand-shake transmission. The dance is accompanied by classical music with lyrics in Malayalam (and English subtitles) addressing the demon and ordering him to leave the house. It is both aesthetic and educational, performing how the demon enters the body and what effects it takes, as well as the hygiene measures of proper handwashing, wearing masks, etc.

SGD Targets


3 (Good Health and Well-Being); 5 (Gender Equality)


Functions

The production is inclusive in presenting translation into English for non-Malayalam/Tamil speakers. It is deliberate in demonstrating coordinated efforts to educate the public in basic hygiene measures to drive away the virus and in delivering a hopeful and optimistic message that the Corona demon is doomed to lose its battle against humans. It is consistent as it is presented on a social media platform
