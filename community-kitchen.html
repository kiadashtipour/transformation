Community Kitchens (India)

This entry deals with various community kitchens started across India amid pandemic to support the poor and needy. The sections in the entry are specified on behalf of the states where it was opened for the people.

Case description


Community Kitchens in Kerala are initiated by the government to provide food free of cost for all those who need it within the state. These community kitchens have been formed in each Panchayat.

SDG Targets

Zero Hunger, Good health and well-being, Gender equality, Reduced inequalities, Partnership, Sustainable cities and communities


Functions

inclusive; deliberate; sudden; top-bottom; progressive

On 10th April 2020 Kerala Government started community kitchens with support from local self-governing bodies in association with kudumba Sree Mission and other volunteer groups. Active participation of public irrespective of gender made this community kitchen a great success in Kerala amid lockdown and hence supports the Gender equality. These community kitchens were opened across Kerala to support migrant workers in Kerala , daily wage workers and elderly people. Their main motive was to avoid starvation amid lockdown. Lots of school kids was depending on the midday meals as a part of midday meal scheme in Kerala, provided by the schools. However, due to lockdown they are deprived of getting midday meals. Through these kitchen government also ensured these kids were given midday meals in packets at their residence. This initiative of government is inclusive because it included poorer and vulnerable sections of the society. This initiative required high coordination and planning hence it is deliberate. It responds to sudden crisis imposed by the lockdown in Kerala. Since the Government of Kerala initiated this with support from local bodies and common people this is a bottom up approach and it also supports the SDG17.It is a progressive approach as it addresses the well being of people at grassroot levels, reduces their exposure to poverty and hunger.
