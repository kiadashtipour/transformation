Covid-19 Transformation: An Experimental Knowledge-Sharing Platform

The COVID-19 Pandemic is transformative on a scale never seen before. The resilience of our societies and communities depends on the nature of the response, for better or for worse. It becomes clear that the pandemic-induced transformations are diverse and uneven, and that scholars and scientists need to improve their communications and their public outreach, with this knowledge-sharing platform offered as a tool to do so. Moreover, unfolding transformation requires documentation of cases around the world and across disciplinary boundaries for the sake of future studies on transformations and their effects on the resilience of social-ecological systems. This website is an experiment in organising knowledge on the varieties of responses and the changes that are currently underway across the world as a step forward to establishing a digital platform for knowledge sharing.

The knowledge on Covid-19 transformation is segmented here into generalised transformation types and related specific case studies. Each type is related to a primary knowledge domain with variations depending on combinations with other knowledge domains. Case studies branch out of the general types and their variations and further linked to several disciplines and sub-disciplines branching out of the knowledge domains. Each case study forms an encyclopedia entry that includes a case description followed by specification of the transformative functions of each case in terms its temporal and spatial qualities on the one hand, and its progression towards or regression from the Sustainable Development Goals (SDG).

Contributors are welcome to add Covid-19 Transformation entries (cases) and to revise and enhance existing entries. Users are welcome to add feedback with suggestions for improvement of the types and the defining categories within each entry.
