This entry includes air medical services and their functioning all over the world amid pandemic.

Royal Flying Doctor Service in Australia for Aboriginals

Case Description

Australiaâ€™s Royal Flying Doctor service undertook the task of providing medical support for corona virus cases in remote areas of Australia. They are travelling miles to treat Covid patients of remote and marginalized communities such as Aboriginals of Australia. They usually travel 16.7 million miles a year to treat patients. However, the pandemic is making their job even tougher.


SDG Targets

3 (Good Health and Well Being); 10 (Reduced Inequalities); Peace Justice and Strong Intuitions

Functions

Inclusive, Deliberate, Sudden, Top-down, Progressive

This act is inclusive as they provided support to the health issues of marginalized and remote communities facing the pandemic. The Royal Flying Doctor Service addressed the health crisis of remote communities by providing medical service irrespective of the risks involved in treating and travelling, hence it is deliberate. It is the sudden action taken by the Service authority. It is a top-down approach as this was initiated by the Australian government health care system. It progresses towards three SDG goals supporting health and wellbeing of communities, reduced inequalities and strong institutions.
